Planetary Registry

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in our solar system. It lacks a significant atmosphere, causing extreme temperature swings between day and night. Its surface is heavily cratered, resembling Earth's Moon, and it orbits the Sun every 88 Earth days, making it the fastest planet in our neighborhood.

Venus

Venus is often called Earth's twin due to its similar size, but it is far more hostile. It has a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a runaway greenhouse effect. It is the hottest planet in the solar system and rotates very slowly on its axis in the opposite direction of most planets.

Earth

Our home planet is the only place in the universe where life is known to exist. It is perfectly positioned in the "Goldilocks Zone," allowing liquid water to remain on its surface. Earth has a complex atmosphere and a protective magnetic field that shields life from harmful solar radiation.

Mars

Mars, the "Red Planet," is a cold desert world with a thin atmosphere. It is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the solar system, and Valles Marineris, a massive canyon system. Scientists continue to explore Mars for evidence of ancient water and potential signs of microbial life.

Jupiter

Jupiter is a massive gas giant, the largest planet in our solar system. It is mostly composed of hydrogen and helium and is known for its Great Red Spot, a storm that has lasted for centuries. Jupiter has a powerful magnetic field and a large family of moons, including the icy Europa.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning and complex ring system made of ice and rock particles. As a gas giant, it is very light for its size. It has over 140 moons, including Titan, which has its own thick atmosphere and liquid lakes of methane, making it a target for future space exploration.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant that appears blue-green due to methane gas in its atmosphere. It is unique because it rotates on its side, likely the result of a massive collision in the distant past. It has a faint ring system and cold temperatures that make it one of the coldest planets.

Neptune

Neptune is the most distant major planet from the Sun and is whipped by supersonic winds. It is a deep blue ice giant with a dark, cold atmosphere. It was the first planet to be discovered through mathematical predictions rather than being seen through a telescope first.
